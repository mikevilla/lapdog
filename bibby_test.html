What is a lap dog?

How do members of the 113th Congress stack up when it comes to supporting legislation to expand background checks to online and gun show sales to keep America safer.

The Lap Dog label is based on two basic criteria:
1. Did you support legislative efforts to expand background checks on gun sales?
AND
2. Did you take money from the corporate gun lobby?
If a member of Congress did not support legislative efforts to expand background checks and took money from the corporate gun lobby then they would be designated as a “lap dog”*.

For the senate, the legislative effort is determined based on how senators voted on the Manchin-Toomey amendment in April of 2013.
  • Thumbs Up means Senator voted for Manchin-Toomey.
  • Thumbs Down means Senator voted against Manchin-Toomey.

For the House, the legislative effort is determined based on whether members have signed on to co-sponsor H.R. 1565 (113th Congress).
  • Thumbs Up means House Member is co-sponsor on H.R. 1565.
  • Thumbs Down means House Member is not co-sponsor on H.R. 1565.
Corporate Gun Lobby Contributions Scale
1 dollar sign equals: $1.00 - $10,000.00
2 dollar signs equal: $10,001.00 - $20,000.00
3 dollar signs equal: $20,001.00 - $45,000.00
4 dollar signs equal: $45,001.00 - $65,000.00
5 dollar signs equal: $65,001.00 - $90,000.00
* Lap Dogs are denoted with a lapdog stencil on their scorecard.

Sources:
For Manchin-Toomey vote.

The corporate gun lobby is defined to include: the National Rifle Association, the National Shooting Sports Foundation and the Gun Owners of America.
