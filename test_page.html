What is a Lap Dog?

A Lap Dog is a member of Congress that takes treats from the corporate gun lobby AND blocks progress on expanding Brady background checks. The rating applies to members of the 113th Congress.

The Lap Dog label is based on two basic criteria:
1. Did you support legislative efforts to expand background checks on gun sales?

AND

2. Did you take money from the corporate gun lobby?

Senators are rated based on how they voted on the Manchin-Toomey amendment legislation in April of 2013 that would have expanded background checks on all online and gun show sales.*
  • A “Thumbs Up” rating means the senator voted for the Manchin-Toomey amendment.
  • A “Thumbs Down” rating means the senator voted against the Manchin-Toomey amendment.

House Representatives are rated based on whether or not they have signed on to co-sponsor H.R. 1565 (113th Congress). H.R. 1565 would have expanded background checks to all online and gun show sales.
  • - A “Thumbs Up” rating means the representative is a co-sponsor on H.R. 1565.
  • - A “Thumbs Down” rating means the representative is not a co-sponsor on H.R. 1565.

The corporate gun lobby is defined to include: the National Rifle Association, the National Shooting Sports Foundation and the Gun Owners of America.

Total money contributions from the corporate gun lobby, dating back to 1990 for the 113th Congress, are based on a dollar sign ($) scale of one to five, and defined as the following:

1 dollar sign equals: $1.00 - $10,000.00
2 dollar signs equal: $10,001.00 - $20,000.00
3 dollar signs equal: $20,001.00 - $45,000.00
4 dollar signs equal: $45,001.00 - $65,000.00
5 dollar signs equal: $65,001.00 and above.

* Two senators, Senator Mark Pryor (D-AR) and Senator Mark Begich (D-AK) are designated as “Lap Dogs in Training” because they voted against the Manchin-Toomey amendment, but have not taken money from the corporate gun lobby.
